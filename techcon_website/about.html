Our History

TechCon began in 2010 as a small gathering of technology enthusiasts in a university auditorium. What started as a modest event with just 200 attendees has grown into one of the world's premier technology conferences.

Over the years, TechCon has evolved significantly. In 2015, we expanded to include international speakers, and by 2018, we were hosting over 5,000 participants annually.

Today, TechCon stands as a testament to the rapid evolution of technology and the power of community in driving innovation forward.

Our Mission

At TechCon, our mission is to bridge the gap between emerging technologies and practical implementation, fostering an environment where innovation meets accessibility.

We believe that technology should serve humanity, and our conference is designed to:

  • Showcase groundbreaking technologies that solve real-world problems
  • Create meaningful connections between innovators, investors, and implementers
  • Promote diversity and inclusion in the tech industry
  • Inspire the next generation of technology leaders
  • Foster ethical discussions around technology development

Through our carefully curated sessions, workshops, and networking events, we aim to create a collaborative space where ideas can flourish and transform into tangible solutions.

Notable Past Speakers

Dr. Elena Rodriguez

Dr. Rodriguez is a leading researcher in AI ethics and algorithmic fairness. She is the founder of the Ethical AI Institute and has advised multiple governments on technology policy.

Marcus Chen

As the CTO of QuantumLeap Technologies, Marcus Chen has been at the forefront of quantum computing research. He holds 15 patents in quantum information processing and has published over 50 research papers.

Dr. Amina Jalloh

Dr. Jalloh is renowned for her work in sustainable technology and green computing. She leads the Green Tech Initiative and has been recognized by the United Nations for her contributions to sustainable development.

James Fitzgerald

With over 20 years of experience in cybersecurity, James Fitzgerald has helped protect critical infrastructure from emerging threats. He currently serves as Chief Security Officer at GlobalSecure Inc.

Dr. Sarah Williams

Dr. Williams revolutionized accessibility technology with her work on inclusive design principles. She directs the Human-Centered Design Lab at Stanford University and has received numerous awards for her contributions to accessibility.
